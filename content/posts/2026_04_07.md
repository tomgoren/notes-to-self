## Democratic work

* Sierra LaDuke with a fun approachable [video essay](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f7iloI8V6Z8)
that tracks discontent of workers through a historic and cultural lens.
* I used to enjoy the podcast
[Economic Update](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLPJpiw1WYdTMLIyASxEheOVjl1vKkajYj)
from [Dr. Richard Wolff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_D._Wolff).
I learned a lot from his advocacy promoting worker owned cooperatives and their
potential transformational power.
His framework also taught me about the economic underpinnings and unseen
incentives that influence so much of world currently and in the past.
That said, my perspective has since evolve to account for forces beyond
dialectic materialism, such as culture, and the fickle nature of the human psyche.
* I've been impressed and inspired by the work being done by
[Geeks For Social Change](https://gfsc.community/the-future-of-geeks-for-social-change/),
an awesome organization out of the UK that is a shining beacon at the
intersection of technology and community.
This is the type of counterbalance we need against the prevalent dystopian
neoliberal capital-fueled techno-optimism that has declared itself "inevitable".

## Music

* Last November I listened to the podcast series
[Fela Kuti: Fear No Man](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/fela-kuti-fear-no-man/id1835735529).
In addition to being a prominent musician and founder of the Afrobeat
genre, he was also a fierce activist of major cultural significance.
I was not aware of the criticism against him and the podcast highlighted both his
contributions and some of his problematic behaviors and opinions.
The series is extremely well produced, and I enjoyed learning about the
historical and familial context that created this singular artist.
